Multi-level Data Exploration Overview¶

Multi-level data exploration refers to the process of examining and analyzing data at different levels of detail or abstraction to uncover patterns, insights, and relationships. It’s like zooming in and out of your data to see both the big picture and the finer details from high-level to low-level exploration.

High-Level Exploration – Look at broad summaries:¶

  • Mean, median, min, max
  • istograms, box plots
  • Count of categories, missing data

Mid-Level Exploration – Compare groups or variables:¶

  • Correlations between variables
  • Group-by aggregations (e.g., average sales per region)
  • Visual comparisons like bar plots or scatter plots

Low-Level Exploration – Dive deep into specific cases:¶

  • Investigating outliers
  • Looking at individual records
  • Tracing data transformations

It’s often used in exploration data analysis (EDA), business intelligence dashboards, or machine learning prep, helping analysts or data scientists move between summaries and specifics to better understand the data.

dates = sorted(df_melt['date'].unique())
def update(frame):
    # To Do: Update the animation frame-by-frame.
    current_date = dates[frame]
    
    for country in countries:
        data = df_melt[
            (df_melt['Country'] == country) &
            (df_melt['date'] <= current_date)
        ]
    
        lines[country].set_data(data['date'], data['Total Deaths'])
        
    return lines.values()
